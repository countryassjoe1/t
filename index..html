Process
How architecture goes from idea to infrastructure.
Every engagement follows a clear path: map, design, deploy, document. No black boxes, no mystery.
Phases
01 — Mapping the system
We define the boundary: what the system touches, who it serves, and what “ten years from now” needs to look like.
02 — Architecture & object model
Entities, flows, contracts, and failure modes are modeled. This is where Codex chapters and protocol specs take shape.
03 — Implementation & automation
Contracts, pipelines, and workflows are implemented with a bias toward determinism and low dependency surfaces.
04 — Documentation & continuity
Everything is documented so a future operator—or AI—can step in without guesswork.

Case Study — Digital Asset Protocol
TenYearsNFT: Time as the value engine.
A duration-based NFT protocol designed to reward patience instead of churn. Holders commit to time, not lockups, and the system tracks and rewards that behavior.
  • Scope: Protocol design, contract architecture, metadata, image pipeline, documentation.
  • Outcome: a fully specified, deployable system with canonical IPFS structure and deterministic generation.

This protocol is built as a legacy artifact—something that can be audited, forked, and extended years from now without losing the original intent.
